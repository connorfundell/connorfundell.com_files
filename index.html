Problem Setup

For a positive integer \(n\), let \[f_n(x) = \cos(x) \cos(2x) \cos(3x) \cdots \cos(nx).\] Find the smallest \(n\) such that \(|f_n''(0)| > 2023\).

Solution

We can first attempt to generalize the first derivative of \[f(x) = \cos(x) \cos(2x) \cos(3x) \ldots \cos(nx)\] using the product rule: \[f_{n}'(x) = \left( \cos x \right)^{'}\left\lbrack \cos(2x)\cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \right\rbrack + \cos{x}\lbrack\cos(2x)\cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx)\rbrack'.\] This simplifies to: \[\hspace{27pt} = - \sin x \cos(2x) \ldots + \cos(x)\left[ \cos(2x)'\left( \cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \right) + \cos(2x)\left( \cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \right)^{'} \right].\]

Repeating this process, we achieve a series of \(f_{n}(x)\) similar terms with one \(- k \sin(kx)\) term replacing the respective cosine term:

\[\begin{aligned} f_{n}^{'}(x) &= \sum_{k=1}^{n} -k \sin(kx) \prod_{\begin{matrix} j=1 \\ j \neq k \end{matrix}}^{n} \cos(jx) \\ &= -\sin(x)\cos(2x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &\quad - 2\cos(x)\sin(2x)\cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &\quad - \ldots - n\cos(x)\cos(2x)\ldots\cos\left( (n - 1)x \right)\sin(nx) \end{aligned}\]

As the first derivative of \(f_{n}(x)\) is a collection of terms similar in form to \(f_{n}(x)\) itself, finding the second derivative is like finding the first derivative \(n\) times:

First term of \(f_{n}^{'}(x)\): \[- \sin x \cos{(2x)\cos{(3x)\ldots\cos(nx)}}\]

Taking the derivative: \[\begin{aligned} \left( -\sin x \cos(2x) \cos(3x) \ldots \cos(nx) \right)' &= -\cos(x)\cos(2x)\cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &\quad + 2\sin(x)\sin(2x)\cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &\quad + 3\sin(x)\cos(2x)\sin(3x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &\quad + \ldots + n\sin(x)\cos(2x)\ldots \sin(nx) \end{aligned}\]

By similar analysis, the second derivative of the second term is: \[\begin{aligned} (-2\cos(x)\sin(2x)\cos(3x)\ldots\cos(nx))' = -2\lbrack &-\sin(x)\sin(2x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &+ 2\cos(x)\cos(2x)\ldots\cos(nx) \\ &- \ldots - n\cos(x)\sin(2x)\ldots\sin(nx) \rbrack \end{aligned}\]

As such, the general form of \(f_{n}^{''}(x)\) looks like: \[- f_{n}(x) - 2^{2} f_{n}(x) - 3^{2} f_{n}(x) - \ldots - n^{2} f_{n}(x) + \text{forms of } f_{n}(x) \text{ containing sine terms.}\]

Evaluating \(f_{n}^{''}(0)\) becomes elementary, as \(f_{n}(0) = \left( \cos 0 \right)^{n} = 1\), and every term containing sine becomes 0. Therefore, \[f_{n}^{''}(0) = - (1^{2} + 2^{2} + 3^{2} + \ldots + n^{2}).\]
